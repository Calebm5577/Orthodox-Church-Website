History

   St. George Orthodox church was founded in 1915 by serbian immigrants. We have always been a relatively small and tight nit community currently serving just under 1,200 people.

   Originally St. George Orthodox church was called St. George Serbian Orthodox Church but this changed in 1960 to remove "Serbian" and keep "St. George". This was done to not scare away newcomers to Orthodoxy as the name implied an ethnic cult despite the meaning only representing the imigirants original country.

   We never wanted to change our Patron Saint from St. George however due to our origins all the way up to the present of being a military community. Our chosen Saint being a military Martyr represents our attitudes as both Soldiers and Christians
